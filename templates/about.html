Filipino appetizers or "Pulutan".

Filipino sisig is a spicy and savory dish made of chopped pig's face and ears (and sometimes liver) served on a sizzling plate, which makes it a popular "pulutan" or appetizer for alcoholic drinks.

Friday is Monggo day!

"Monggo" refers to the mung bean, a key ingredient in a popular Filipino comfort food called Ginisang Monggo, a savory stew of sautéed mung beans with garlic, onions, tomatoes, and leafy greens like spinach or malunggay.

Filipino food. It’ll blow your mind.

Filipino food is a diverse cuisine influenced by Malay, Chinese, Spanish, and American cultures, known for its sweet, sour, salty, and savory flavors.

Filipino cuisine is a vibrant and diverse culinary tradition that reflects the country's rich cultural history and regional variations. It offers a wide range of flavors and cooking techniques, from simple meals to elaborate dishes, and continues to evolve and adapt to new influences and contexts.
